Description

Becoming New Me Plus (BNM+) uses cognitive behaviour therapy. It is for men who are at high or very high risk of reoffending. Suitable for men convicted of a general violence offence.

Who is eligible

This programme is for men who have:

  • a conviction for general violence offence (GV)
  • a high or very high risk of reoffending
  • a learning disability or challenge (LDC)
  • a High OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • a High OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)

Becoming New Me Plus is the Learning Disability and Challenges equivalent of the Kaizen programme.

Outcomes

By the end of the programme, participants will be able to:

  • understand the underlying causes of their violent behaviour, for example past trauma or emotional dysregulation
  • use techniques to manage anger, like relaxation or breathing exercises
  • replace unhelpful beliefs that lead to violence with more positive ways of thinking
  • identify high-risk situations and plan ways to avoid them
  • understand the principles of healthy relationships, including how to set boundaries

Session details

Participants will be eligible for either:

  • 7 one-to-one sessions and 85 group sessions
  • 20 one-to-one sessions
