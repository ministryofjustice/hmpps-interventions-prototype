Stepwise Driving

Criteria

Gender
Male or female
Type
Structured Interventions
Needs
Education, training and employability
Risk criteria
OGRS score of more than 25%
Time to complete
At least 12 months
Format
One-to-one or group
Attendance type
In person or online

Description

Stepwise Driving is a structured intervention for people in England and Wales who have been convicted of driving while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

It focuses on areas such as awareness of safe driving, like making decisions and consequential thinking.

Outcomes

By the end of the programme, participants will be able to:

  • understand how to drive safely for example, what is a safe reaction time and stopping distance
  • improve their decision-making skills by understanding the risks and consequences of driving under the influence
  • learn about alternative methods of transport they can use during their disqualification period
  • understand their legal obligations

Session details

The programme is made up of:

  • an initial one-to-one session
  • 5 group sessions
