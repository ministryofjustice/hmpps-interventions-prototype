Description

This service helps men with all of the following rehabilitative needs:

  • emotional wellbeing
  • family and significant others
  • lifestyle and associates
  • social inclusion

Who is eligible

To be eligible, men must be:

  • leaving prison before the end of their sentence
  • on probation or under supervision after release from prison
  • on a community sentence or suspended sentence with a rehabilitative activity requirement (RAR)

Details

The service may include:

  • in-person or online activities
  • support and advocacy
  • advice and guidance

A social inclusion ‘mentoring’ service is available pre-release for men on probation or under supervision. If the appointments are part of the sentence, they are enforceable.

Outcomes

Personal Wellbeing services are designed around participants’ needs. They aim to achieve one or more of the following outcomes:

  • a stronger, more positive sense of self and access to community support groups or organisations
  • spending time doing purposeful activities and building positive relationships
  • reduced engagement with people involved in crime and criminal activities
  • relying less on negative peer relationships or networks, such as organised crime groups or extremism organisations
  • being active in their community and able to make a positive contribution
  • positive family relationships and avoids harmful relationships are developed or maintained
  • confident and responsible parenting behaviours are demonstrated (where applicable)
  • improved ability to develop positive intimate relationships including communication, resilience, negotiation and assertiveness skills
  • positive coping strategies in the event of temporary or irretrievable breakdown of familial or other relationships are demonstrated
  • compliance with any voluntary or mandatory family or relationship-focused therapeutic/behavioural change programmes
  • coping skills and strategies to regulate mood and demonstrate perspective-taking and self-care by avoiding risky situations
  • feeling more confident capable and resilient
  • ability to access and engage with mental health services
  • ability to recognise and manage triggers to worsening wellbeing
  • making and keeping positive social interactions
  • compliance with any required treatment, therapy or medication plans
  • building supportive social networks to feel less isolated
  • connecting with community-based services soon after release from prison
  • developing strength and determination to cope with challenges and barriers when returning to the community
