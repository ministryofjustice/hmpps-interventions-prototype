Description

Accommodation services provide pre-release support to help people find and keep housing. These services are designed to help people in prison and on probation address their accommodation needs and achieve stability.

Who is eligible

To be eligible, men must be:

  • leaving prison before the end of their sentence
  • on probation or under supervision after release from prison
  • on a community sentence or suspended sentence with a rehabilitative activity requirement (RAR)

The Personal Wellbeing service covers a variety of interventions and outcomes based on an individual's risk category. This can be low, medium or high.

Outcomes

Accommodation services are designed around participantsâ€™ needs. They aim to achieve one or more of the following outcomes:

  • removing barriers to finding or keeping a home, like money problems, behaviour, health or past offences
  • supporting participants to find and secure social, supported or private accommodation
  • preventing homelessness, including support to help stop an eviction
  • stable housing for at least 3 months, including for men who have been in prison for less than 6 months
