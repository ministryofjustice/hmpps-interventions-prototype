Description

Help is an early intervention, domestic abuse programme. The aim is to support the development and maintenance of healthy intimate relationships and to protect against the escalation of problematic behaviours.

To do this, it offers insight into the values, attitudes and skills which are necessary to develop and maintain healthy intimate relationships. It provides the opportunity for the man referred to reflect on his attitudes and behaviours within the relationship and to work towards positive change.

Outcomes

By the end of the programme, participants will be able to:

  • develop and maintain healthy intimate relationships
  • protect against the escalation of problematic behaviours

Eligibility and suitability criteria

Criminogenic needs score

At least 1 of the following 3 OASys criteria needs to be satisfied:

  • score of 2 on item 11.3 - aggressive behaviour
  • answer of ‘yes’ to ‘Thinking and behaviour’ being related to risk of serious harm to self or others
  • answer of ‘yes’ to ‘Attitudes’ being related to risk of serious harm to self or others

Offence type the person was convicted for

Intimate partner violence (IPV) - eligibility is based on the offence factor of ‘domestic abuse’.

Suitability for the programme

You should consider the following information before making a referral to this intervention:

If there are less than 9 months remaining on licence or community order, you should check with the Treatment Manager.

Any alcohol or drug-related problems must be stable before attending the programme.

People with mental health problems should only be excluded from the programme if the issues are acute and damage their ability to relate to others or concentrate.

The person you are referring must not be suitable for, or be unable to access an Accredited Programme.

The person you are referring must be able to read and understand simple sentences.

This programme is only suitable for heterosexual men.
