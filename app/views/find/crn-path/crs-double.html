Overview (Intervention summary)

The Personal Wellbeing service is a holistic service that looks to address the following rehabilitative needs emotional wellbeing, family and significant other, lifestyle and associates, social inclusion. The Personal Wellbeing service is aimed at male individuals on a community or suspended sentence order with a RAR or on license/post-sentence supervision. The Social Inclusion ‘mentoring’ service will be delivered pre-release for individuals who will be on license. The Personal Wellbeing service provides a variety of interventions and outcomes based upon the individual’s risk category (low / medium / high).

What they will study (Topics/curriculum)

A range of approaches will be used including:

  • direct delivery of interventions
  • support and advocacy
  • advice and guidance

Interventions will be tailored to reflect the complexity of an individual Service User’s needs.

Interventions will be tailored to reflect the complexity of needs and should aim to secure one or more of the following outcomes for Service Users:

  • improved pro-social self-identity and ability to access community-based support networks
  • sustained engagement in pro-social leisure interests and purposeful activities
  • reduced engagement with pro-criminal associates and activities
  • a decreased reliance on negative peer relationships or networks, such as organised crime groups or extremism groups.
  • engaged in their community and able to make a positive contribution.
  • positive family relationships and avoids harmful relationships are developed or maintained.
  • confident and responsible parenting behaviours are demonstrated (where applicable).
  • Improved ability to develop positive intimate relationships including communication, resilience, negotiation and assertiveness skills.
  • positive coping strategies in the event of temporary or irretrievable breakdown of familial or other relationships are demonstrated.
  • compliance with any voluntary or mandatory family or relationship-focused therapeutic/behavioural change programmes.
  • compliance with any medication/treatment/therapy programmes
  • social networks to reduce initial social isolation are developed or sustained
  • early post-release engagement with community-based services is secured.
  • resilience and perseverance to cope with challenges and barriers on return to the community is developed

These will support the development of:

  • coping skills and strategies to regulate mood and demonstrate perspective-taking and self-care by avoiding risky situations.
  • levels of self-efficacy, resilience and confidence
  • ability to access and engage with mental health services.
  • ability to recognise and manage triggers to worsening wellbeing.
  • ability to build and maintain appropriate social interactions.
