Overview (Intervention summary)

Stepwise Emotions is a structured intervention. A structured intervention is a set of exercises delivered by a regional interventions team to help participants work towards positive life goals and deal with situations in ways that lead away from offending. Structured interventions help participants build on strengths they already have and develop new skills for a better future. Exercises enable them to manage challenging thoughts and emotions, work through problems, and build better relationships with people in their life. Structured interventions may be completed as part of a community order, suspended sentence order or as a licence condition

Stepwise Emotions helps participants to manage their thoughts, feelings and behaviour towards others. It combines accredited programme (AcP) content with content from legacy interventions developed by community rehabilitation companies. Completing each session can be classed as one day of a rehabilitation activity requirement.

Who is eligible

You can refer for this intervention if all of the following apply:

  • they are both male and female
  • they are aged 18 or over
  • If case is not suitable for an AcP, then OVP: 20+ (If No OVP, OGRS: 25+)
  • Need is evidenced by at least one of the following OASys criteria being satisfied, 11.3 = 2, Thinking & behaviour related RoSH, Attitudes related RoSH

What they will study (Topics/curriculum)

Stepwise Emotions consists of a total of 34 exercises delivered across 9 sessions. It can only be accessed by participants who have completed a pre-intervention module (or equivalent set of activities) with their Probation Practitioner (or other nominated supporter) to help get them ‘group room ready’. Stepwise Emotions can only be mandated therefore for participants with 10 or more RAR days

Modules

  • Preliminary One-to-One Session (including Introducing Stepwise Emotions, Understanding Me, Life Map, Portfolio)
  • Foundation (includes Steps Towards Change, Identity, Role Call, Accessing to Community Supports: Survival Packs, Risk and Protective Factors - Goal Setting, Success Wheel, Tug of War, Understanding What I Do: Self-Monitoring & the Learning Log, Power of Breathing)
  • Emotions (including Understanding Stress, Triggers, Managing Stress, Time Out)
  • Thinking (including Seeing It As It Is, Reading Situations (Thinking Biases), Seeing the Bigger Picture (Helicopter View Tool), Here and Now Practice, Self-Talk, Personal Rules, Challenging Unhelpful Thinking (The RUN tool), The Great Eight)
  • Safe & Social (including Social Circles, Getting into the Right Frame of Mind to Talk, Communication Skills (The BRITE Tool), Being Assertive – Getting Yourself Across, Skills Practice, Dealing with Criticism, Staying on Track, Change, Accept, Let Go, Skills Practice, Affirmation Wheels, My Steps Towards Change, Reslience and Change, Moving Forward

Goals aiming to achieve

  • group session - build prosocial behaviour, review thoughts or questions from the previous session and get participants ready for learning.
  • exercises - developing a working alliance with the participant and strengthen your working alliance by giving the participant an opportunity to describe aspects of their learning history that they feel comfortable sharing.
  • Although we don’t aim to address unresolved trauma on this intervention, we know that participants are affected by their past and we will create a supportive place to strengthen coping skills.
  • The Staying on Track skill aims to help give participants a greater sense of control over their thinking, emotions and behaviours when they are being pressurised by peers to do something against their interests.
